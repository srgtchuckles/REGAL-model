## Summary

GPS Phase 1 trial in AML patients in complete remission (CR) with detectable WT1 transcript. Very small n (10 enrolled, 9 evaluable) but shows the same pattern as Phase 2: high CD4+ and CD8+ response rates, 5/9 alive without disease at follow-up, median DFS not reached.

## Key Claims

### Patient Population
- n=10 enrolled, n=9 evaluable
- AML in complete remission (CR) with detectable WT1 transcript by PCR
- Median age 64 years (range 22–76)
- Median CR duration before vaccination: 10 months (range 5–16)

### GPS Dosing
- 4 WT1 peptides at 200 µg each + GM-CSF adjuvant
- 6 vaccinations over 12 weeks (weeks 0, 4, 6, 8, 10, 12)
- 3 patients received up to 12 total vaccinations

### Immune Response Rates
- **CD4+ response: 7/8 tested = 87.5%** (strong proliferative responses to immunizing peptides)
- **CD8+ response: 3/3 HLA-A*02:01+ patients** — all showed:
  - Significant IFN-γ-secreting cells by ELISPOT
  - Increased WT1 tetramer-positive CD8+ T cells
  - Robust expansion of WT1-specific CD8+ T cells after 3 vaccinations
- DTH responses: 3/9 patients positive post-vaccination

### Clinical Outcomes
- **Median DFS: not reached**
- **Median OS: 35+ months for entire cohort**
- **4/9 patients relapsed** (after doses 3, 6, 8, and during treatment); all subsequently died
- **5/9 patients alive without disease** at follow-up (mean 30 months from diagnosis, range 18–41)
- WT1 transcript levels remained stable at low levels during remission; large increases preceded clinical relapse — suggesting GPS may suppress MRD

### Safety
- Minimal toxicity (all Grade <2)
- Primarily local injection site reactions
- 2 hypersensitivity events (1 generalized urticaria after 5th dose; 1 GM-CSF reaction)

## Relevance to REGAL Outcome Prediction

Phase 1 shows the same pattern as Phase 2:
- High immune response rates (CD4+ ~87.5%, CD8+ 100% of HLA-A*02:01+ patients)
- 5/9 (55%) alive without disease — directionally consistent with the Phase 2 DFS plateau of ~57.5%
- Median DFS not reached

The consistent signal across Phase 1 and Phase 2 increases the plausibility that the Phase 2 cure fraction signal is real, not a noise artifact. Two independent small cohorts showing the same pattern is stronger evidence than one alone.

**MRD finding is notable:** WT1 transcript suppression during vaccination may mean GPS is actively controlling MRD — a mechanistic correlate of the clinical cure fraction signal.

## Contradictions / Tensions

- n=9 in Phase 1 is even smaller than Phase 2 (n=22). Both are underpowered.
- The CD8+ response rate in Phase 1 is 100% of HLA-A*02:01+ patients (3/3), slightly higher than Phase 2's 86% (6/7). Small n artifacts can inflate these numbers.

## Open Questions

- Were Phase 1 patients in CR1 or CR2? (Disease setting matters for REGAL extrapolation)
- Is there longer follow-up data from the Phase 1 cohort?
- What were the WT1 transcript dynamics in the 5 long-term remission patients?
