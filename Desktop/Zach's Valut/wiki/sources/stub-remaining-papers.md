## Scheibenbogen 2002 — CD8 T-cell responses to WT1 and PR3 in AML

**Citation:** Scheibenbogen C, et al. Blood. 2002 Sep 15;100(6):2132-7. PMID: 12200377.

**Expected content:** Early demonstration that AML patients can mount WT1-specific CD8+ T-cell responses without vaccination — establishing WT1 as an immunogenic target. Context for why GPS's mechanism is plausible.

**Priority:** Medium — background biology.

## Keilholz 2009 — Phase 2 of WT1 peptide vaccine in AML and MDS

**Citation:** Keilholz U, et al. Blood. 2009 Jun 25;113(26):6541-8. PMID: 19389880.

**Expected content:** Phase 2 of a different WT1 vaccine in AML and MDS. Clinical and immunologic outcomes. Useful comparator to GPS Phase 2 — same antigen, different formulation. May have response rate and durability data.

**Priority:** High — direct comparator to GPS.

## Berlin 2015 — HLA ligandome landscape of AML

**Citation:** Berlin C, et al. Leukemia. 2015 Mar;29(3):647-59. doi: 10.1038/leu.2014.233. PMID: 25092142.

**Expected content:** Systematic mapping of HLA-presented peptides on AML blasts. Should show what fraction of AML patients' blasts present WT1-derived peptides, and on which HLA alleles. Critical for understanding how many REGAL patients' leukemic cells are actually targetable by GPS-induced T cells.

**Priority:** High — validates the target (WT1 presentation on AML blasts in CR2 patients).

## Zirlik 2006 — Heteroclitic peptides and primary tumor killing

**Citation:** Zirlik KM, et al. Blood. 2006;108(12):3865-3870. doi: 10.1182/blood-2006-04-014415.

**Expected content:** Shows that T cells raised against a heteroclitic (modified) peptide can kill primary tumor cells expressing the native peptide. This is the key biological validation for GPS's use of heteroclitic WT1-A1 — T cells trained on the vaccine's modified peptide should still kill native WT1-expressing AML blasts.

**Priority:** Medium — validates heteroclitic peptide strategy.

## Assudani 2007 — CD4+ T-cell help in cancer immunity

**Citation:** Assudani DP, et al. Cancer Immunol Immunother. 2007;56(1):70-80. doi: 10.1007/s00262-006-0154-6.

**Expected content:** Review/analysis of why CD4+ T-cell help is essential for durable cancer immunity. Mechanistic basis for why GPS's CD4+ response arm (via long peptides) may be critical for sustained remission. Relevant to interpreting the "none of the CD4+ responders relapsed" finding from Phase 2.

**Priority:** Medium — mechanistic background for CD4+ durability.

## Lim & Zainal 2021 — Monitoring T-cell responses from cancer vaccines

**Citation:** Lim KP, Zainal NS. Front Mol Biosci. 2021 Apr 15;8:623475. PMID: 33937323; PMCID: PMC8082312.

**Expected content:** Review of methods for measuring T-cell responses to cancer vaccines (ELISPOT, tetramer, proliferation assays). Methodological context for interpreting the Phase 2 immune response data — particularly whether the assays used (ELISPOT, tetramer) are reliable enough to support the cure fraction inference.

**Priority:** Low — methodological background.
