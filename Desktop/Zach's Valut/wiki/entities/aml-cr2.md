## Overview
AML in second complete remission (CR2) refers to patients with acute myeloid leukemia who achieved a complete remission after first relapse, having previously achieved and lost a first complete remission (CR1). This is a high-risk population with poor long-term prognosis under standard therapy.

## Relevance to Outcome Prediction
The baseline prognosis of AML CR2 sets the floor for the BAT arm. If historical OS in CR2 is very poor, even a modest GPS benefit could be statistically detectable. If the BAT arm performs unexpectedly well (e.g., due to allo-SCT or novel therapies), GPS needs a larger effect to show significance.
